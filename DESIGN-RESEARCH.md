# Design research: Website Design Outsource

## Search intent

Query used: `outsource web design services`

The buyer is usually an agency owner or marketing lead with more web work than the in-house team can comfortably ship. They need a quiet production partner, not another public-facing agency competing for the client relationship. The homepage should answer four questions quickly: what can the team take on, how will the handoff work, who owns the files, and what happens before a page is called finished.

## Chosen direction

Superside is the strongest visual reference because it makes the work visible immediately. Its dark evergreen canvas, editorial headline treatment, cropped website mockup, and lime conversion color feel like a design business rather than a staffing template.

The new direction borrows those principles, not the trade dress:

- a deep ink-green studio canvas with warm paper sections
- a custom browser-board composition built around a relevant design-team image
- a small serif accent inside a mostly sans-serif type system
- one sharp lime action color
- a useful service rail at the bottom of the hero
- process details and file-ownership language instead of invented client logos, ratings, savings, or project counts

## Originality notes

The homepage uses an original composition, copy, logo, color values, card geometry, browser mockup, and section rhythm. No competitor assets, copy, logos, testimonials, metrics, or distinctive illustrations are reused. The design deliberately avoids public pricing and unverified proof. Its central idea is a “quiet production desk”: the client sees the agency, while the agency sees a clear brief, review trail, and handoff.
